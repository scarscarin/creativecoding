Course Details

Course Description

In Creative Coding, code becomes both the tool and the craft that supports your creative practice.

When designing interactive media, code exists at various levels: as the engine of your work, as the material forming and informing your process, and/or as the critical subject of your research.

This course introduces the different languages and practices of Creative Coding, with a method that is applied, creative, and critical. The classes are designed to encourage collaborative, open-ended, inclusive, and sustainable approaches to computing in art and design.

No previous knowledge of coding is required.

Learning Objectives

  • You will learn to use code as both a functional and expressive mean to support your creative practice
  • You will learn to be autonomous in reading, debugging, and researching code-related tasks
  • You will learn how to collaborate with fellow coders or to direct a group project which involves a level of coding
  • You will grow a critical perspective in regard to the cultural aspects of code

Teaching Methods

Every week the class focuses on a different assignment and generates an artistic response, gradually transitioning from small in-class workshops to a longer-term autonomous project.

Along with the tutorials, the course runs a field mapping program, inclusive of theory lectures, group discussions, and an off-site visit to a media art institution.

If you come more than fifteen minutes later (without warning), or leave earlier (without my clearance), it will count as an absence.
